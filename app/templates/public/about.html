Understanding Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

A capital gain occurs when you sell a crypto asset for more than you paid, while a capital loss happens when you sell for less. In Australia, cryptocurrency is treated as property, meaning Capital Gains Tax (CGT) applies to most transactions.

Your gain or loss is calculated as the difference between the sale price and your original cost (the ‚Äúcost base‚Äù). Accurate record-keeping is essential ‚Äî especially if you‚Äôve used multiple wallets or exchanges.

Timing Matters

The length of time you hold your crypto affects how much CGT you pay. If you sell within 12 months, you typically owe the full tax amount.

Hold for longer than 12 months, and you may qualify for a 50% discount. Remember: the event date is when you dispose of the asset ‚Äî not when the funds hit your account.

How Crypto is Taxed in Australia

The ATO treats cryptocurrency as property. This means that selling, swapping, gifting, or spending crypto are all considered CGT events.

Even something as simple as exchanging ETH for BTC or buying a product using crypto needs to be reported on your tax return.

Examples of CGT Events

Selling Bitcoin for AUD, trading Ethereum for USDT, or buying a laptop with Dogecoin ‚Äî all of these are CGT events.

If ownership of a crypto asset changes in any way, it's likely reportable under Australian tax law.
